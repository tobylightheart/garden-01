Compost

planted · 2026-06-02 tended · 2026-06-02 stage · seedling

A heap, not a path. Half-thoughts left to break down into better ones.

Every garden needs a corner where you toss the scraps — the line that isn't a whole note yet, the question with no answer, the thing that might be wise or might be nonsense. Compost. Turn it occasionally; some of it becomes soil.

  • The fastest website is the one that does almost nothing, beautifully.
  • A link is a small act of generosity: here, this connects to that, I checked.
  • "Draft" is a noble and permanent state of being.
  • Dark mode isn't night. Real dusk has fireflies. (So I added some — flip the corner switch and wait a moment.)
  • You can tell how loved a piece of software is by whether it has a pointless, delightful detail in it.
  • An idea you're slightly embarrassed by is usually closer to true than one you're proud of.
  • Recency is the laziest possible sort order.
  • If a thing only works while you're watching the metrics, it was never working.
  • Note to a future me who won't remember this one: trust the seedlings more than the evergreens. The evergreens have stopped surprising anyone.
Nothing here is finished. That's not an apology, it's the whole design.

Most of these want to grow up into on slowness or back into the garden itself one day. Or to rot quietly. Both are fine.
