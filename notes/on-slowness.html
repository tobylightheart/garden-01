On slowness

planted · 2026-06-02 tended · 2026-06-02 stage · seedling

A seedling. Rough, and that's allowed.

A page like this one loads instantly, runs no tracker, phones no home, and will work in twenty years if a browser can still open a file. It is slow in the good sense: it was made slowly, by hand, and it asks nothing of you in a hurry.

I think there's a quiet pleasure in software that doesn't try to capture you — no infinite scroll, no badge counts, no "you might also like." Just a thing that sits there being itself until you choose to look.

Half-formed thoughts I want to grow here later:

  • Slow ≠ inefficient. This is the fastest page in the garden because it does almost nothing.
  • The web used to default to documents. Documents are calm. Apps are loud.
  • What would a "seasonal" website look like — one that changes with the actual year, not with engagement?
Note to self: don't over-tend this one too soon. Let it stay a seedling for a while.

Grows alongside tending, not publishing.
